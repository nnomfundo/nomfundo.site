S500 Quadcopter Drone Drawing

This page showcases the mechanical design of an S500 quadcopter drone, modeled using SolidEdge. The focus is on structural balance, arm integration, and aerodynamic layout. Explore the visual breakdown below.

Initial Frame Drawing

The initial drone frame included triangular cutouts in the arms to reduce weight. However, I later learned that sharp corners can become stress concentration points, which may weaken the structure under load. This insight led me to revise the design to improve durability without compromising on weight efficiency.

Mid-Stage Assembly

This drawing shows the S500 quadcopter frame with additional structural elements, including cylindrical placeholders and a layered plate setup. It represents the next step in the modeling process, where the flat frame begins to take on a 3D form to suggest where components like motors or landing gear would be positioned.
