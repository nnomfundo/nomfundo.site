S500 Quadcopter Drone Drawing

This page showcases the mechanical drawing of an S500 quadcopter drone, modeled using SolidEdge. The aim of this project was to sharpen my CAD skills and gain a deeper understanding of structural design principlesâ€”particularly the importance of optimizing the strength-to-weight ratio in mechanical components.

Initial Frame Drawing

The first stage involved creating a symmetrical base structure with strategic cutouts to reduce material usage while maintaining structural integrity. This pushed me to think critically about load paths, symmetry, and the role of geometry in minimizing weight without compromising strength.

The initial drone frame included triangular cutouts in the arms to reduce weight. However, I later learned that sharp corners can become stress concentration points, which may weaken the structure under load. I later added 1mm fillets in all sharp corners as seen in the mid-stage assembly.

Mid-Stage Assembly

The second stage involved assembling components on the frame, which helped reinforce my knowledge of 3D modeling tools like extrusion, offsetting, and alignment. I had to ensure that each part was properly positioned and constrained within the overall structure, which required careful use of lofting, patterning, and reference planes.
